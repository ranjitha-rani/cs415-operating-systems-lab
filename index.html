LABORATORY

Spring 2026

Every computer you use—from your laptop and phone to the servers powering the internet—relies on an operating system working quietly in the background. It is the layer that makes hardware usable, allows multiple applications to run at once, manages memory and storage, and keeps systems stable and responsive even under heavy use. Have you ever wondered how your system switches smoothly between apps, protects one program from interfering with another, or keeps running when many tasks compete for attention? Understanding operating systems helps reveal the hidden structure that makes modern computing possible.

Syllabus

Objectives:

In this course, we will explore how operating systems work behind the scenes to manage hardware resources and support modern applications. The course will help students develop understanding of the following broad topics:

  • Understanding the role and structure of operating systems and their interfaces with hardware and applications.
  • Process management and concurrency, including scheduling and synchronization.
  • Memory management techniques such as paging, segmentation, and virtual memory.
  • File systems, storage organization, and device management.
  • Deadlocks, resource allocation strategies, and system performance considerations.
  • Foundations of operating system security and protection mechanisms.
